Overview

Abstract

VGGT-HPE studies head pose estimation through the lens of relative pose prediction. Rather than regressing an absolute pose from a single image in isolation, the project treats head pose as a geometric relation between observations. This framing is meant to better exploit correspondence, pairwise structure, and the inductive biases of geometry-aware vision backbones, while aligning the task more naturally with projective reasoning. This page will host the final manuscript, code, and qualitative results as those materials are released.

Why Relative Pose?

Relative formulations expose the geometry of the task directly and avoid reducing the problem to a disconnected absolute-angle regression target.

Why VGGT?

Geometry-grounded visual features are a natural fit for pose reasoning, especially when the model is asked to compare views instead of memorizing canonical head angles.
